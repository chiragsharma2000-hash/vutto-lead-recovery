The buyer journey

How a buyer actually moves — and where they fall out

The buy-side funnel, mapped stage by stage. Acquisition channel is stamped at the very first touch and decides how you win them back at the end.

1

Request to buy

Customer raises intent to buy a bike — via the site, an ad, WhatsApp or a referral.

Channel stamped here
2

Sales call

The sales team calls the lead to qualify intent and book a visit.

3

Qualify → visit or drop

Real requirement → invited for an in-person viewing. Weak lead → drops off.

Leak #1: weak leads drop
4

Showroom visit

They inspect & test ride — but may not buy, for many reasons (price, EMI, condition, financing, family…).

Leak #2: no-purchase
5

Convert — or recover

They buy, or they leave. Whether you win them back depends on the channel they came from.

Agent plugs in here
4→5

The expensive leak is Stage 4→5. You've already paid to acquire the lead, qualify it and get the person physically into a showroom — the most expensive part of the funnel. When they walk out without buying and the reason lives only in a sales rep's Excel notes, that entire investment is written off. This is the moment the agent captures, structures and re-activates.

Problem

Offline showroom feedback never becomes a CRM signal

The CRM is great at the online funnel. But the richest intent data — what a real human said in the showroom about why they didn't buy — is trapped in Excel and rep memory.

✕

Reasons stay unstructured

"Exchange value too low", "EMI felt high", "wants to check with family" — written as free text, never tagged, never queryable.

↺

No systematic recovery

A hot lead who was ₹2,000/mo away from buying is treated the same as a tyre-kicker. No score, no cohort, no next-best-action.

◎

Channels fly blind

You can't tie a walk-in's outcome back to the campaign that produced it — so retargeting and ad spend are guesswork.

Root cause · the channels

The real driver is the acquisition channel

Every lead is born on a channel, and the channel predicts intent, the likely objection, and how to win them back. Group them into two families — then act on the difference.

Organic · high intent, low cost
Walk-inReferralOrganic search / SEOGoogle Business & MapsInbound WhatsAppRepeat / word-of-mouth

Self-motivated buyers. Objections skew practical — price, condition, paperwork. Recover with trust & a small nudge; protect this channel, it's your cheapest CAC.

Inorganic (paid) · volume, variable intent
Meta AdsGoogle Search AdsYouTube / DisplayInfluencerLead-gen aggregatorsOffline / OOH

Higher volume, noisier intent. Objections skew financial — EMI, financing rejection, "just browsing". Recover with finance options & urgency; measure each campaign's true cost-per-recovered-sale.
